Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 17, 2026

At DropBeam, accessible from https://dropbeam.com, one of our main priorities is the privacy of our visitors. This Privacy Policy document contains types of information that is collected and recorded by DropBeam and how we use it.

If you have additional questions or require more information about our Privacy Policy, do not hesitate to contact us at support@dropbeam.com.

1. General Data Collection

DropBeam does not collect, record, or request any Personal Identifiable Information (PII) during your usage. We do not require registration, email validation, names, or passwords to access our sharing boards.

When you visit our website, our server logs your local IP subnet interface in temporary memory solely to group your browser connection with other devices on the exact same Wi-Fi router. We do not write your IP addresses to any persistent database.

2. Shared Data & Files

Any text or file that you share through DropBeam is treated as temporary data:

  • Files: Files uploaded to our server are saved in a temporary folder and are automatically deleted after exactly 15 minutes. Additionally, you can choose to enable "Self-Destruct" to purge files immediately upon their first download.
  • Live Clipboard & Chat: Clipboard contents and chat messages are held strictly in temporary memory (RAM) and are deleted automatically when you overwrite them, clear them, or when all room clients disconnect.

We do not screen, index, or parse the content of files or text clips that you share. It is your responsibility to ensure you have the legal right to share any uploaded content.

3. Cookies and Web Beacons

Like any other website, DropBeam uses "cookies". These cookies are used to store information including visitors' preferences, such as your UI dark/light theme setting, sound muting choice, and last active sharing room ID. This configuration is stored locally inside your browser's Local Storage to preserve your preferences across page refreshes.

4. Google DoubleClick DART Cookie & Advertising Partners

Google is one of the third-party vendors on our site. It also uses cookies, known as DART cookies, to serve ads to our site visitors based upon their visit to https://dropbeam.com and other sites on the internet. However, visitors may choose to decline the use of DART cookies by visiting the Google ad and content network Privacy Policy at the following URL: https://policies.google.com/technologies/ads.

Some of the advertisers on our site may use cookies and web beacons. Our advertising partners include:

  • Google AdSense (Privacy Policy: https://policies.google.com/technologies/ads)

These third-party ad servers or ad networks use technologies like cookies, JavaScript, or Web Beacons that are used in their respective advertisements and links that appear on DropBeam, which are sent directly to users' browsers. They automatically receive your IP address when this occurs. These technologies are used to measure the effectiveness of their advertising campaigns and/or to personalize the advertising content that you see on websites that you visit.

Note that DropBeam has no access to or control over these cookies that are used by third-party advertisers.

5. GDPR Data Protection Rights (EEA)

We want to ensure you are fully aware of all of your data protection rights. Every user is entitled to the following under GDPR:

  • The right to access: You have the right to request copies of your personal data. (Since we store no personal data, any request will return null).
  • The right to rectification: You can ask us to correct any information you believe is inaccurate.
  • The right to erasure: You can ask us to erase your personal data under certain conditions.

6. CCPA Privacy Rights (California)

Under the CCPA, among other rights, California consumers have the right to:

  • Request that a business disclose the categories and specific pieces of personal data that a business has collected about consumers.
  • Request that a business delete any personal data about the consumer that a business has collected.
  • Request that a business that sells a consumer's personal data, not sell the consumer's personal data.

7. Children's Information

Another part of our priority is adding protection for children while using the internet. We encourage parents and guardians to observe, participate in, and/or monitor and guide their online activity.

DropBeam does not knowingly collect any Personal Identifiable Information from children under the age of 13. If you think that your child provided this kind of information on our website, we strongly encourage you to contact us immediately and we will do our best efforts to promptly remove such information from our records.
